The Curse of Bush Honeysuckle

Written by David Pierson

Facts about Bush Honeysuckle

  • Originated from Eurasia
  • It produces bright red berries
  • It can changed the chemistry in the soil
    • Low in fat, high in carbohydrates
    • Berries start to produce around Fall

How we can help prevent the spread of Bush Honeysuckle

  • Removing Bush Honeysuckle and other invasive species from your property
  • Joining local volunteer groups
  • Methodology includes: Cut and treat, Foliar and Basal Bark treatment
  • Filling empty space with Native plant species

About me

I have personally been active in treating and removing Bush Honeysuckle for the past 4 years. I've done this by volunteering with a conservation group, and now working with a Native Landscape company. I've quickly realized that this specific invasive species has made home in most urban and natural areas. Even though it will take several years to push it back to a acceptable level, it is still worth the fight. Educating the public about this shrub is critical to being able to knock it down. Invasive species will always be around but if we don't do anything about it, they will push out and kill our native species. This could be very detrimental to our ecosystem.
